Full-Body Model in OpenSim

I built this simplified full-body human musculoskeletal model using OpenSim 4.4 and OpenSim Creator. The model is based on the Rajagopal 2015 musculoskeletal framework and incorporates Millard 2012 equilibrium muscle models. My goal is to iteratively expand this foundation into a more anatomically comprehensive and functionally rich system.

This project demanded a strong focus on anatomical accuracy and precision in joint and muscle path placement. It broadened my perspective on how computational tools can be leveraged to analyze human movement.

Sensory Signal Classification

My Master’s thesis in biomechanics focused on exploring how sensory stimuli applied to the fingers can be decoded from brain signals by utilizing magnetoencephalography (MEG) and support vector machines. We recorded MEG data from participants as they received stimuli to their fingers, then analyzed these signals and tried classification between the types of stimuli and the fingers stimulated.

Through this research, I developed expertise in MEG data processing and machine learning applications. The project deepened my understanding of the integration of interdisciplinary knowledge from neuroscience, biomechanics, and computer science.

Gait Analysis with MATLAB

These are short scripts that perform simple gait analysis on motion capture (.c3d) and IMU data, such as identifying gait events and calculating toe clearance and gait asymmetry. Toe clearance, for example, is an important gait metric that can predict the risk of falls in the elderly.

Here I learned to apply my theoretical knowledge of biomechanics and signal processing to real-world data. I also gained a better understanding of the importance of careful study design, data collection, and error tracking to produce clean and noise-free data.

My First Scientific Publication

This project was done in collaboration with the Centre of Military Medicine in Finland and it also served as my bachelor's thesis. The goal of this project was to develop and test a portable neck strength device for fighter pilots. Together with my supervisors we were able to get this study published as a technical note in Aerospace Medicine and Human Performance.

Throughout this project, I developed the skills necessary to carry out a research project from start to finish. It strengthened my ability to work independently, take initiative, and manage each stage of the process—from planning and data collection to analysis and final presentation.
