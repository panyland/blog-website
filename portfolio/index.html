Master's Thesis

My Master’s thesis in biomechanics focused on exploring how sensory stimuli applied to the fingers can be decoded from brain signals by utilizing magnetoencephalography (MEG) and support vector machines. We recorded MEG data from healthy participants as they received stimuli to their fingers, then analyzed these signals and tried classification between the types of stimuli and the fingers stimulated.

Through this research, I developed expertise in MEG data preprocessing and machine learning applications. The project deepened my understanding of neuroimaging techniques and the integration of interdisciplinary knowledge from neuroscience, biomechanics, and computer science.

Gait Analysis with MATLAB

This is a short script that identifies gait events and calculates minimal toe clearance from motion capture data (in .c3d format). It uses markers located in the anterior superior iliac spine, heels and toes. Toe clearance is an important gait metric in the elderly as it gives indications about fall risk.

Here I learned the importance of tracking measurement errors and attention to detail. Small errors in data collection or analysis might cumulate to a significant degree which is why it is important to carefully design and carry out experiments.

My First Scientific Publication

This project was done in collaboration with the Centre of Military Medicine in Finland and it also served as my bachelor's thesis. The goal of this project was to develop and test a portable neck strength device for fighter pilots. Together with my supervisors we were able to get this study published as a technical note in Aerospace Medicine and Human Performance.

Throughout this project I learned the importance of perseverance and adaptability. Furthermore, the project fostered my ability to work autonomously, taking ownership of my research and embracing the challenges that came with it.
